MapReduce Matrix Computations

with Professor David Gleich (Purdue), Professor James Demmel (UC-Berkeley), BeBOP group (UC-Berkeley, LBNL)

We are currently working on Tall and Skinny QR (TSQR) and Tall and Skinny Cholesky QR (TSCQR) factorizations in MapReduce frameworks. This work has looked at data serialization, programming language implementations (C++, Python), numerical stability, and performance with faults. Most work has used the Hadoop cluster on NERSC's Magellan cloud testbed.

The software for this project is available on GitHub:
  • David's repository: https://github.com/dgleich/mrtsqr
  • My development fork: https://github.com/arbenson/mrtsqr
I have completed two reports and two posters for this project:
  • Math 221 report (Fall 2011)
  • Math 221 poster (Fall 2011)
  • CS 267 report (Spring 2011)
  • CS 267 poster (Spring 2011)

Gamma-ray Imaging Framework (GRIF)

with Dr. Dan Chivers (LBNL), Dr. Mark Bandstra (UC-Berkeley, LBNL), Bearing group (UC-Berkeley, LBNL, SSL), DoNuTS (UC-Berkeley), ANP group (LBNL)

The Bearing group is an interdisciplinary effort by students and researchers in nuclear engineering, (astro)physics, mathematics, electrical engineering, and computer science that works on problems in nuclear detection. The Bearing group is a part of the Domestic Nuclear Threat Security Initiative (DoNuTS) at UC-Berkeley.

Currently, the group is developing a parallel, multi-platform software framework for gamma-ray imaging that is used for real-time nuclear detection. The project is designed to be flexible enough for researchers outside of our group to adopt as a common model for detector software support. My work has included data analysis, backend architecture, and code generation techniques for the project.

The software was open-sourced in May 2012: https://github.com/bearing/grif
